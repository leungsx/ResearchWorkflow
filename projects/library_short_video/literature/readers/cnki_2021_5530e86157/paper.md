## Reading Notes

- Summary after guided source-grounded skim: This paper treats short-video marketing as an important route for public libraries to promote digital reading in the all-media era. It argues that short videos can expand the coverage and depth of digital reading promotion by adding contextual, interactive, and immersive service layers to digital reading texts. The paper studies public-library Douyin head accounts with more than 10,000 followers and summarizes their practice into three experience patterns: high-quality PUGV content that activates users' social attributes, a "dual matrix" account/channel strategy, and community/scenario-based interaction built on user tags. It then translates these patterns into three strategy lines: emotional marketing, channel marketing, and interactive marketing.
- Innovation / value after guided source-grounded skim: Its main value is not statistical causal testing, but service-strategy synthesis. Compared with papers that only diagnose low adoption or list popular content traits, this paper connects library short videos to digital reading promotion, cross-platform channel design, user-tag-based targeting, online-offline conversion, and sustained participation. It is useful for shifting our project from "how to get likes" toward "how short videos support library reading-promotion services."
- Method details after guided source-grounded skim: The author uses Douyin as the observed platform and searches with `公共图书馆`, `阅读推广`, and `数字阅读`. After half-year dynamic tracking from 2020-05-01 to 2020-11-30, the paper selects 90 public-library Douyin head accounts whose follower counts remained above 10,000, and reports that these accounts published 27,455 short videos during the observation period. The analysis focuses on image scenes, IP marketing, brand planning, copywriting, interaction strategy, content, mode, and path. It is best read as descriptive practice extraction and strategy induction, not regression or causal identification.
- Usable evidence block IDs: `B0001` for abstract, contribution and three strategy lines; `B0003`-`B0005` for research gap, head-account definition, sample criteria, time span and 90-account / 27,455-video scope; `B0005`-`B0007` for three practice patterns; `B0008`-`B0013` for emotional marketing, channel marketing, interactive marketing, online-offline conversion and POI / digital reading examples.
- Limitations and uncertainty: The paper focuses on head accounts, so it may overrepresent better-resourced public libraries and underrepresent ordinary, small, inactive, university, or research-library accounts. The analysis is mostly descriptive and strategy-oriented; it does not test whether the proposed emotional/channel/interactive strategies independently improve engagement, service uptake, or reading outcomes. Its platform snapshot is 2020-era Douyin, and some table values may require page-level checking against the original PDF/CAJ before being cited. The outcome language still leans toward attention, participation, and marketing reach, so later work should connect it to measurable service value and reading-promotion outcomes.
