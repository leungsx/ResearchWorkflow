Reading Notes

  • Summary after guided companion reading: This is an early status-problem-countermeasure paper on library Douyin accounts. It frames short-video platforms as important mobile-Internet traffic entrances, then uses a 2019 Douyin search for official physical-library accounts to describe low account adoption, weak influence, irregular operation, and lack of original high-quality content. It proposes platform entry, professional account management, library-resource-based original content, and online-offline interaction as core countermeasures.
  • Innovation / value after guided companion reading: Its value is mainly baseline diagnosis rather than theory testing. Compared with later empirical-factor papers, it gives an early field snapshot: official library Douyin accounts were still sparse and weak in 2019, and library short videos were understood primarily as a new publicity/service channel. This helps explain why later studies shifted toward content factors, interaction indices, and service-effect evaluation.
